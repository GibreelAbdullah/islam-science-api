PROOF FOR GOD'S EXISTENCE

Classical Islamic Theologians have explained two rational proofs for the existence of God. These are:


Proof 1: The Contingency Argument

It can be written in simple deductive logic format as:

Minor premise: The universe exists Contingently.

Major Premise: Anything that exists Contingently needs a Necessary Being to make it the way that it is.

Conclusion: The universe needs a Necessary Being to make it the way that it is.

There are several online resources that explain the Contingency Argument, including:

• Very short explanation by Shaykh Hamza Karmali, The Quranic Contingency Argument in Less Than 5 Minutes YouTube Video

• Longer explanation by Shaykh Hamza Karmali, Part of Why Islam Is True YouTube series


Proof 2: The Kalam Cosmological Argument

It can be written in of simple deductive logic format as:

Minor premise: The universe began to exist.

Major Premise: Everything that began to exist was made to exist by something that did not begin to exist.

Conclusion: The universe was made to exist by something that did not begin to exist.

There are several online resources that explain the Kalam Cosmological Argument, including:

• Explanation by Shaykh Hamza Karmali, Part of Why Islam Is True YouTube series

• Shaykh Hamza Karmali, All God's Prophets Made the Kalam Cosmological Argument


The above proofs remain valid irrespective of developments in science or other knowledge domains, as these proofs are based only on basic rationality and logic. Anyone who thinks rationally necessarily arrives at these conclusions.


The above proof shows that a Necessary Being exists. Classical Islamic Theologians provide a list of 13 important Attributes for the Necessary Being:

  1. Existence
  2. Not Beginning
  3. Not Ending
  4. Self-subsistence, meaning not needing any place or determinant to exist.
  5. Dissimilarity to Created Things
  6. Uniqueness, meaning having no partner (sharik) in His entity, attributes, or actions.
  7. Omnipotent Power
  8. Will
  9. Knowledge
  10. Life
  11. Hearing
  12. Sight
  13. Speech

Classical Islamic Theologians also provide the proofs for all these Attributes. The proof for existence is the Contingency Argument and Kalam Cosmological Argument mentioned above. The proofs for other attributes are included in classical books of Islamic Theology and are taught all over the world in Islamic Seminaries (Madrassas).

The Necessary Being with these 13 Attributes is God (or Allah) in Islam.
