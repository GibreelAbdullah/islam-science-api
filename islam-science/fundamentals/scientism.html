Scientism is the philosophical stance that science is the ultimate authority on all questions, extending its methods and principles beyond natural phenomena to areas addressed by the humanities, ethics, metaphysics, and religion. While science excels at finding correlations in the physical world and driving technological advancement, scientism's overreach leads to an unfounded dismissal of moral values, non-physical experiences, and religious practices. Scientism attempts to reduce complex human realities to empirical data and disregards the insights provided by philosophy, theology, and spirituality. This reductionist approach fosters an overly mechanistic worldview, limited to materialistic explanations. By failing to recognize the limits of science, scientism undermines its own credibility and neglects the importance of interdisciplinary dialogue.


Dr. Rik Peels classifies scientism into weak scientism and strong scientism. He explains weak scientism as: “Weak scientism says that only science provides knowledge in a particular realm of life. Examples are metaphysical intuition, introspection and morality.” He explains strong scientism as: “Strong (rather than weak) scientism says that only science provides knowledge in any realm, including all the above realms. That is also how the American philosopher of science Massimo Pigliucci understands the term ‘scientism’: it is ‘a totalizing attitude that regards science as the ultimate standard and arbiter of all interesting questions; or alternatively that seeks to expand the very definition and scope of science to encompass all aspects of human knowledge and understanding’. One might think that this is an absurd view that no one accepts. But several scientists and philosophers call themselves adherents of scientism and explicitly embrace this view.”


Prof. Austin L. Hughes writes: “Advocates of scientism today claim the sole mantle of rationality, frequently equating science with reason itself. Yet it seems the very antithesis of reason to insist that science can do what it cannot, or even that it has done what it demonstrably has not. As a scientist, I would never deny that scientific discoveries can have important implications for metaphysics, epistemology, and ethics, and that everyone interested in these topics needs to be scientifically literate. But the claim that science and science alone can answer longstanding questions in these fields gives rise to countless problems.”


The way science is presented in many popular science writings and videos has led to the spread of scientism among the general public. Studying philosophy of science can help a lot in countering such false notions. It can be hoped that there will be more focus on the philosophy of science in the future, which will help in spreading a proper understanding of science among the general public.
