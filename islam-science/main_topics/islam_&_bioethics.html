ISLAM & BIOETHICS

Islam and bioethics field focuses on applying Islamic theological principles and Islamic jurisprudence (fiqh) to address contemporary medical and healthcare issues. It examines topics such as end-of-life care, organ transplantation, reproductive technologies, genetic engineering, and public health from an Islamic perspective. Scholars who work in this field aim to integrate modern medical practices with Islamic moral teachings, ensuring that healthcare practices match the religious guidance provided by Islam. The field has seen a great deal of development in the last decades with interesting dialogues between Islamic scholars, medical professionals, and bioethicists.

 

The field is one the most developed compared to other topics in Islam, Science, and Technology. Islamic Scholars in the past decades have issued numerous fatwas in response to fiqh questions related to medical practices.  Numerous books and articles have also been published in the past decades discussing various aspects on modern medical practices. However, there is still scope for further studies on specific issues that are complex and have been differed over like organ transplantation. 

 

Dr. Aasim I. Padela is currently one of the most prominent researchers who works in Islam and bioethics field. He has been able to provide unique contributions to the field. His contributions include:

 

  1. Islamic Bioethics Frameworks: Dr. Aasim has been instrumental in developing systematic frameworks for bioethics from an Islamic perspective. He emphasizes the importance of applying classical Islamic jurisprudence (fiqh) to solve contemporary biomedical dilemmas in issues like end-of-life care, organ donation, and reproductive health.

 

  1. Healthcare Equity for Muslims: His work investigates the healthcare experiences of Muslim patients in diverse contexts, highlighting religious factors that influence their interactions with medical professionals. He advocates for culturally competent medical care that respects religious beliefs and practices of Muslims.

 

  1. Research on Religious Coping: Dr. Aasim has studied how religious and spiritual coping mechanisms affect health outcomes and decision-making among Muslim patients.

 

  1. Education and Capacity Building: He has worked extensively on educating healthcare providers about Islamic ethical principles, fostering dialogue between Islamic scholars and medical professionals to address ethical dilemmas in clinical practice.

 

Overall, Dr. Aasim Padela's work is very noteworthy and has helped greatly in the development of this field. Dr. Aasim has published extensively in peer-reviewed journals, addressing topics within Islam and bioethics.

 

Shaykh Dr. Rafaqat Rashid has published several books/articles which are related to this field. His works What invalidates Fasting Related to the Throat and Bodily Cavities In light of Modern Medicine, Revising the Fiqh of Khamr and Alcohol: Ethical Use from an Islamic Perspective, and The Fiqh of Contraception and Abortion offer unique contributions to this field.

 

Al Balagh Academy frequently runs courses on various topics related to Islam, medicine, and medical technologies. An on-demand course is available on Al Balagh Academy specifically on Islam and Bioethics.

 

An academic book titled Islam and Biomedicine has been published in 2022. The book is edited by Dr. Afifi al-Akiti and Dr. Aasim I. Padela and consists of contributions from Islamic Scholars and experts on bioethics. The book begins with the first chapter titled ‘Taking on the Ghazālian Challenge of Integrating Religion and Science in Islam and Biomedicine’. The book is then divided into three sections each with four chapters. The first section is titled ‘From Greek Sources to Islamic Conceptions of Health and Biomedicine’. The second section is titled ‘The Meaning of Life and Death’ and has four chapters. A chapter by Shaykh Hamza Yusuf which discusses when a human foetus actually becomes a human. Another chapter by Shaykh Dr. Rafaqat Rashid discusses the intersection of science, Sunni theology, and Islamic fiqh in defining medical death. The third section of the book is titled ‘Interfacing Biomedical Knowledge and Islamic Theology’. An interesting chapter by Dr. Aasim Padela assesses Maṣlaḥa and Ḍarūra in Islamic Bioethics and the Case of Organ Donation.

 

 

Another recent book on the topic is ‘Medicine and Shariah’ edited by Dr. Aasim Padela. It consists of contributed chapters on various topics including insights from fatwa literature, principle of Darura (Dire necessity) and its applications, determination of death, and perspectives related to American healthcare system.

 

The Initiative on Islam and Medicine (II&M) is a research center at the intersection of Islam and Biomedicine. II&M focuses on improving the health of American Muslims, and on developing an academic, multidisciplinary field of Islamic Bioethics. The Initiative conducts scholarship, provides educational opportunities, and hosts events that bring together medical and social scientists, Islamic studies experts, and traditional Islamic scholars for interdisciplinary dialogue and research. Dr. Aasim I. Padela is the President and Director of Initiative on Islam and Medicine. They hold workshops, conferences, online and offline courses related to Islam and Bioethics. They also have ongoing research projects, an upcoming book, and several publications.
